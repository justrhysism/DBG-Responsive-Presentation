Deeper Borrowing

Another word we've borrowed whether we're familiar with it or not:

Canvas

Photo by Cara StHilaire

Canvas

  • A painter selects the size and type of canvas they wish to work with.
  • A sculptor chooses a size and type of stone to start from.

Immediately provides a boundary for the work to come.

Defining our Canvas

Flawed Process

Imagine a painter selecting their canvas, finishing their work, and then handing their work off to someone to cut into pieces and try to glue it all back together on a canvas of a different shape and size.

Which then changes for each person who looks at it...

Constraining

We define our canvas in Photoshop, immediately establishing constraints:

  • Fixing layout width, assuming minimum screen resolution
  • Defining type in pixels
  • Fixing height on certain elements

Eventually, the design is approved. We then immediately cut it to pieces and try to shove the design into our real canvas.

The Browser: Our Real Canvas

A canvas which:

  • Can be any size
  • Be on many different devices
  • Display fonts differently
  • Even display colours differently depending on the screen

But we've always done it that way...

Our current process is comfortable because it's what we know.

We like setting constraints and boundaries; it gives us something to work with.

The problems:

  • Many browsers
  • More devices

  • Even more users, and their individual:

    • Font settings
    • Screen sizes
    • Display colours and quality
  • The list does go on...

Fluid Design

Fluid Design Also known as “Liquid” design.

Width of the design is the width of the screen no matter the width.

As the width of the screen reduces, the content “flows” to fit.

These designs are based on percentages to determine widths.
